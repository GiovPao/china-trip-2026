📅 Today
  • 🏯Morning: Temple of Heaven (天坛) — the most perfect example of Ming Dynasty architecture. Go early (07:00–08:00) to watch locals doing Tai Chi, dancing, playing erhu in the parks.
  • 🌳Afternoon: Summer Palace (颐和园) — a vast imperial garden with Kunming Lake, Long Corridor (painted walkway), Marble Boat. Allow 3 hours. Rent a rowboat on the lake.
  • 🦆Evening: Peking Duck dinner — if not done yesterday. Quanjude (全聚德) near Qianmen is the most famous. Book ahead.

📝 My notes

Notes & reminders:

Summer Palace entry: ~¥30. Temple of Heaven: buy the combined ticket (~¥35), not the basic one — the basic ticket doesn't include the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (祈年殿), the main building everyone comes to see. Multiple reels flag this as a classic tourist mistake.


📍 Temple of Heaven route (from reels): No advance reservation needed — scan QR code at the gate. Go early for locals doing Tai Chi, dancing and playing music in the cypress tree park surrounding the buildings.


📍 Summer Palace route (from reels): Enter from the East Gate → Hall of Benevolence and Longevity (仁寿殿) → walk the lake perimeter → climb Longevity Hill (万寿山) → exit from the North Gate. All main highlights, no backtracking.


📍 From the reels: The ITA reel says the best photo angle of the Hall of Prayer at Temple of Heaven is from the southern cypress alley, not the main entrance path. Go at 07:00 — the early morning Tai Chi and music sessions by locals are described as one of the most memorable moments of a Beijing trip.
