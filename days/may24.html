📋 Book in advance

Pre-book Forbidden City tickets: released at 20:00 Beijing time exactly 7 days ahead (May 17). Set an alarm. Closed Mondays. Buy via Trip.com or official WeChat mini-program.

📅 Today
  • 🏯Tiananmen Square (天安门广场) — the world's largest public square. Free but requires passport reservation 1 day ahead. Arrive early for fewer crowds.
  • 🏯Forbidden City (故宫博物院) — enter from the south (Meridian Gate, 午门). Allow 3–4 hours minimum. The Imperial Garden at the north end is beautiful and quieter.
  • 🌆Jingshan Park (景山公园) — directly north of the Forbidden City. Climb to the top for the iconic panoramic view over the golden rooftops. ~2 CNY entry.
  • 🛍️Afternoon: explore Wangfujing pedestrian street or Nanluoguxiang hutong for shopping and snacks.
  • 🍛After Jingshan Park, try Jianbing (Chinese crepe) from a street cart on Wangfujing — the reels recommend it as the best quick street food in this area.

Forbidden City ticket: ___________.


📍 Crowd hack (from reels): Don't queue at the main Tiānānmén gate. Two alternatives: (1) Enter via the side gate of Sōngshān Park (松山公园) — costs ¥2, puts you inside by 8:30. (2) Take the subway to Jǐyú Hútòng station, walk along the moat past Dōnghuámén (东华门) — scenic, quiet, bypasses the main chaos entirely.


📍 Inside the palace (from reels): Skip the crowded central axis. Go straight to the Imperial Garden (御花园) and the side pavilions — that's where the real beauty and tranquillity are. After exiting north, climb Jǐngshān Park (景山公园) for ¥2 — panoramic view over the entire golden roofline. The 2-day local reel calls this unmissable.


📍 Afternoon food: Try Ròu jiā mó (肉夹馍) — meat in flatbread — from a street cart near Wángfǔjǐng. For Peking Duck tonight, the ENG food reel specifically recommends Sìjì Mínfú (四季民福) over the tourist chains — book ahead.


📍 From the reels: Enter Tiananmen from the south (Qianmen side). The creator of the ENG reel specifically says to explore the side halls of the Forbidden City (less crowded and more interesting than the central axis). The north end Imperial Garden is highlighted as a hidden gem worth 30 extra minutes.
