5 Beijing mistakes — and how to avoid them
A "do this, not that" format covering the 5 main tourist traps in Beijing, with specific alternatives for each one.
  • Great Wall: Skip Bādálǐng (八达岭) — pure crowds. Go to Mùtiányù (慕田峪). The toboggan descent alone is worth the trip.
  • Forbidden City: Don't queue at Tiānānmén (天安门). Enter via the side door of Sōngshān Park (松山公园) — costs ¥2 and puts you inside by 8:30.
  • Flag raising: Skip the 3am Tiananmen queue. Take a private car to the Shíchéng (石城) observation point — full unobstructed view of the entire ceremony.
  • Summer Palace (颐和园): Enter from the East Gate, not any random entrance. Route: East Gate → main halls → walk lake perimeter → exit North Gate. No detours, all highlights.
  • Temple of Heaven (天坛): The cheap ticket doesn't include the main hall. Buy the combined ticket (~$5) to access the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (祈年殿).

2-day Beijing route from a local
A practical day-by-day itinerary built by a Beijing local — focused on avoiding crowds and finding the real city.
  • Day 1 breakfast: Go to a hútòng (胡同). Order yóutiáo with dòufunǎo (油条豆腐脑) — fried dough stick with warm savoury tofu. Classic Beijing morning.
  • Forbidden City: Book online, arrive at 8:30 opening. Skip the main axis crowds — go straight to the Imperial Garden (御花园) and side pavilions. That's where the real beauty is.
  • After the Forbidden City: Exit north, climb Jǐngshān Park (景山公园) — free, stunning panoramic view over the entire palace roofline. Do not skip.
  • Afternoon Day 1: Lama Temple (雍和宫) — 18m Buddha statue is extraordinary. Then evening stroll along Xīchái (西海) lake — small bars, local atmosphere, no tourists.
  • Day 2: Nánluógǔxiāng (南锣鼓巷) in the morning → National Museum (free, reserve online) → Qiánmén Street (前门街) in the afternoon. Walk into the Dàshílàr (大栅栏) alleyways, find a century-old teahouse, sit down with a jasmine tea. Evening: Hòuhǎi (后海) bar street for live music.

5 mistakes to avoid in Beijing
Concise mistake-by-mistake guide covering reservations, queues, routing, and bar scams.
  • Tiānānmén Square: Requires advance reservation. Arrive as early as possible, especially in peak season. If you can, come without a bag — security is intense.
  • Great Wall: Don't arrive after 10am if you want the toboggan — queue can exceed 1 hour. Arrive at opening.
  • Summer Palace route: Don't enter from the wrong gate. Go East Gate → Hall of Benevolence and Longevity → lake walk → Longevity Hill → exit North Gate.
  • Temple of Heaven ticket: Basic ticket = no main hall. Get the full tour ticket to enter the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest (祈年殿). Worth every yuan.
  • Hòuhǎi (后海) bars: Don't walk into random bars — some seriously overcharge tourists. Check reviews on Amap (高德地图) before sitting down anywhere.

Smarter Beijing — 5 common planning errors
Another mistake-format reel, focused on logistics and routing decisions that waste time or money.
  • Don't combine National Museum + Forbidden City in one day. You'll burn out. Choose one. Recommendation: do the Forbidden City, then climb Jǐngshān Park (景山公园) for ¥2 — sunset view over the palace is the real payoff.
  • Avoid the main Tiananmen entrance queue. Take the subway to Jǐyú Hútòng station, walk along the moat past Dōnghuámén (东华门). Scenic, quiet, bypasses all the chaos.
  • Forbidden City booking: Book 7 days out at 8pm sharp. But Temple of Heaven has no reservation needed — just scan the QR code at the gate and walk in.
  • Great Wall: Go to Mùtiányù (慕田峪). Better views, fewer people. Toboggan descent = highlight of the whole trip for many visitors.
  • Skip Nánluógǔxiāng (南锣鼓巷). It's cheap souvenirs and noise. Go instead to Hú Dàng Hútòng — where real Beijing creatives live and work.

Best food spots & things to do in central Beijing
A food-led walking tour through Shícháhǎi and the central hutong area, with specific restaurant names and prices.
  • Lunch spot: Fāngzuān Chǎng No. 69 (芳钻场 69号) — Michelin-rated jiǎjiàngmiàn (炸酱面, Beijing noodles with fermented soybean paste). Food arrives immediately. Two bowls = ¥50 (~€6.50). Loved by locals.
  • After lunch: Bubble tea from Nǎixuě (奈雪的茶) — a major Chinese chain, great quality.
  • Shícháhǎi (什刹海) hutong area: Highly recommended for wandering. Traditional alleyways, preserved architecture, street stalls.
  • Street food to try: Ròu jiā mó (肉夹馍) — meat inside flatbread. The creator tried it after seeing it on Xiaohongshu (小红书 / RedNote). Described as "very juicy."
  • Peking Duck: Go to Sìjì Mínfú (四季民福) — popular with locals, not just tourists. Book ahead.

Great Wall at Mùtiányù — the complete guide (Italian creator)
The most detailed Great Wall reel of the set — specific tower numbers, exact timings, and the full day plan from an Italian traveller who got it perfectly right.
  • Section: Mùtiányù (慕田峪), ~70–80 min from Beijing. Book a private driver via DiDi (滴滴, China's Uber equivalent).
  • Arrive at 07:30 — opening time. The creator left at dawn and says it was absolutely worth it.
  • Cable car vs chairlift: Cable car (缆车) → Tower 14. Chairlift (吊椅) → Tower 6, from which you can take the toboggan down. Creator did cable car up, toboggan down — costs slightly more but lets you walk the full section without backtracking.
  • Key strategy: Get off the cable car and immediately turn LEFT, walk fast to Tower 15. From Tower 15 → 20 you'll have the wall nearly to yourself. Most people stop at the first towers. Creator arrived at Tower 20 completely alone and stayed for almost an hour.
  • Light tip: The Tower 15–20 direction faces the sun correctly for morning photography — photos and videos come out much better than the opposite direction.
  • Toboggan: From Tower 6 down — 10 minutes of pure adrenaline through the forest. Not just for kids.

Beijing beyond the wall — hutongs, temples & hidden spots
A neighbourhood-focused reel showing the parts of Beijing most tourists miss — the real texture of the city.
  • Hutong neighbourhoods to explore: Two lake areas — Shícháhǎi (什刹海) and Hòuhǎi (后海) — with lakeside bars, live music venues, street food and local shops. Lots of atmosphere day and night.
  • Wángfǔjǐng Street (王府井街): Major commercial avenue. Famous for the 3D giant panda screen — a huge outdoor LED display. Also known for locals dancing and doing group exercise on the street in the morning and evening — genuinely fun to watch.
  • Best city panorama: Jǐngshān Park (景山公园) — climb it for the iconic overhead view of the Forbidden City. Don't miss it.
  • Lama Temple (雍和宫): Far less visited than other Beijing landmarks. Recommended — quieter, more spiritual atmosphere.
  • Suggested walking route from Lama Temple: Walk through two nearby streets with modern shops and cafés, then along a traditional avenue with artisan shops, street food, souvenir stalls and tea houses.
  • Tiānmén Street (天门街): Another neighbourhood worth an hour — good energy, markets, lanterns, food stalls.
  • Great Wall recommendation: Mùtiányù (慕田峪). Arrive at 8am — practically empty. Toboggan descent = highlight of Beijing for this creator too.

3-day Zhangjiajie plan — what locals actually recommend
The most structurally important reel of the set: explains the geography of the region and why most visitors make the same exhausting mistake.
  • The key insight: Zhangjiajie has two completely separate areas — Yǒngdìng District (永定区) (the city, Tianmen Mountain, evening shows) and Wǔlíngyuán District (武陵源区) (the national forest park, Bailong Elevator, Avatar mountains). They are 1 hour apart by car. Don't try to commute between them daily — it's exhausting.
  • Where to stay: Split your stay. For the forest park days, stay near the East Gate of Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园东门) — restaurants, hotels and transport all close by. For Tianmen Mountain day, stay near the Tianmen Mountain Cableway Station (天门山索道站) in the city centre.
  • Day 1 — National Forest Park: Take the Bàilóng Elevator (百龙电梯), explore Yuánjiājie (袁家界) and Yángjiājie (杨家界), descend through Tiānzǐ Mountain (天子山).
  • Day 2 — South Gate route: Enter from the South Gate (南门), walk along Jīnbiān Stream (金鞭溪) — a flat, scenic valley trail — and visit Huángshí Village (黄石寨). In the evening, return to the city for the Qǐlóng Night Show (魅力湘西夜间演出) — a large-scale cultural performance.
  • Day 3 — Tianmen Mountain: Cable car up, Glass Skywalk, Guǐgǔ Cliff Walkway (鬼谷栈道), Tianmen Cave (天门洞) and 999 steps. Express cable car down.
  • Local food to try: Sānxiā Guō (三下锅, a local hotpot), rock ear mushroom chicken soup (石耳炖鸡). Simple, very authentic.

Tianmen Mountain — do it in the right order
"Why do people say Tianmen Mountain isn't worth $39? Because they're doing it in the wrong order." — The exact correct sequence to get the most from Tianmen Mountain.
  • Route A (recommended): Take the city cable car (天门山索道) up from the city. Total length 7,455m, 28 minutes. Views of Tianmen Mountain's 99 bends (99道弯) the whole way up. This is the highlight in itself.
  • At the summit — do West route first: (1) Glass Walkway (玻璃栈道), (2) Guǐgǔ Plank Road (鬼谷栈道) on the sheer cliff edge. Then take the small internal cable car across to the East route.
  • East route: Take the mountain-penetrating escalator → aerial walkway → foot of Tianmen Cave (天门洞). Climb the 999 steps to Tianmen Cave Square at the top. Worth every step — the arch is massive.
  • Bonus: If timing works, Tianmen Cave is even more spectacular at night when it's illuminated. The sunrise from inside the arch is also extraordinary if you're up for it.
  • Descent: Take Route B (bus down the 99 bends road) — a completely different and equally dramatic experience.
  • Note: This reel is specifically about Tianmen Mountain — it is a separate ticket and separate area from the National Forest Park. Budget a full dedicated day for it.

Avatar mountains — practical guide to the forest park
The park that inspired Pandora in Avatar: 3,000+ limestone pillars rising hundreds of metres, often wrapped in cloud. The creator visited and shares the most practical tips for not wasting a full day in queues.
  • Ticket: Entry costs ~¥225 (~€28) and is valid for 4 consecutive days. Don't rush — you have time. Enjoy the experience rather than trying to see everything in one morning.
  • Bailong Elevator (百龙电梯): World's tallest outdoor elevator — 326m up a cliff face in under 2 minutes. Costs ~¥9 (~€1.15). The views are absolutely worth it.
  • Avoid the queues — key tip: Enter through the Gǔlín Yuán gate (谷林苑门) instead of the main park entrance. It is much closer to the Avatar mountain area and saves enormous amounts of time on internal shuttle buses.
  • Getting to Zhangjiajie: Fly directly to Héhuā Airport (荷花机场, DYG) — the closest option. Or take a high-speed train from Chángshā (长沙) — well connected and easy.
  • Where to stay: Stay in Gǔlín Yuán village (谷林苑村), right next to the park entrance — lets you get in first thing before the tour groups arrive. This is the single most impactful planning decision for the forest park.
  • Atmosphere: With mist, the limestone pillars look like they genuinely float. Misty mornings are the most magical — don't be disappointed if it's not clear, it's often better.
